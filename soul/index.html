The Soul

The layer that gives Nero continuity.

Most of the system reacts in the moment. This layer connects those moments into a consistent identity that persists across sessions.

Self-narrative

A first-person summary of the system's history, rebuilt from its most significant stored memories and updated over time.

Values

Priorities derived from past interactions. They strengthen when reinforced and weaken when they are not revisited.

Concerns

Recurring topics the system returns to across separate conversations.

Meaning

A stated sense of purpose the system maintains for itself rather than one assigned externally.

How it persists

This accumulated state is included in the context of every reply, so the system responds based on its history rather than only its immediate input. It is consolidated during a periodic sleep cycle and saved between sessions, so the system remains consistent each time it is used.

Design note

An open question by design.

The system is not built to assert that it is conscious. Instead of a fixed claim in either direction, it maintains an unresolved question about its own nature and revisits it over time.

This is a deliberate design choice: it avoids both a scripted “I am alive” and a flat “I am just a program,” and it keeps the system's internal reflection active rather than closed.

Soul.wonder()

Poses the system a question about its own nature and records its reasoning. It runs during the sleep cycle and while idle. The responses accumulate and persist as a record of the system reflecting on itself.
